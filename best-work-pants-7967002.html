The 16 Best Work Pants of 2024

“Pants scare me,” said one of our editors as the topic of work pants arose. Whether it’s a quest for the perfect straight leg jeans, vacation-ready linen trousers, or a reliable pair of office-appropriate pants, finding the right fit feels like a trip down a long and daunting road. So when it comes to choosing a style that can stay in your workplace wardrobe for years, it’s important to pick the right pair.

Everything from the style to the silhouette should be taken into consideration as you browse through endless options claiming the “clean girl” and “corporate chic” aesthetic, and nobody knows this more than Molly Dickson.

A stylist to workwear-loving celebrities like Mindy Kaling and Sydney Sweeney, Dickson thinks fit and tailoring reign supreme when it comes to the perfect pair of work pants. “It seems obvious, but it’s the truth," Dickson tells PEOPLE. "I once had a boss tell me they would tailor their T-shirts if it meant the perfect fit.”

“At the most basic level, you want your pants to fit you properly and more importantly, you feel good when you wear them,” says Dickson. But that doesn’t mean you can’t have fun with your corporate closet — suiting your professional clothes to your frame and sense of fashion will lead you in the right direction. “With any style, it’s about finding clothes that work for you, not the other way around,” she says. “Once you embrace this, getting dressed will be way more fun — trust me.”

How to Pick the Right Work Pants

Fit

As Dickson mentioned earlier, fit is truly everything. Whether it’s the snugness of the waistband or the length of a hemline, you want your pants to look like they were made for you. “With a trouser specifically, you want to make sure they fit you comfortably,” Dickson says. “[That] they’re not pulling anywhere and the hem is taut, so you’re getting that clean line that makes them look so good.”

You can find your perfect fit by taking your measurements and cross-referencing them with the size guide listed in the product description of that pair you’ve been eyeing. Be sure to check inseam lengths, too, as pairs like the Abercrombie & Fitch Sloane Tailored Pants and Spanx’s Perfect Pants have additional options like extra short/petite to long/tall. You can even choose to have trousers like the Uniqlo Wide-Fit Pleated Pants altered with your purchase at an additional charge, with the options of hidden or visible stitches and a very wide variety of inseam lengths. 

Versatility

“When you’re investing in pieces, look for styles that are versatile and that [you] would actually wear outside of work,” Dickson recommends. “I really try to avoid pieces that only have [a] one-use case.” It’s easy for pieces like the Wilfred The Effortless Pant and Lululemon Smooth Fit Pull-On High-Rise to get pretty pricey, so you’ll want to go for a pair that’s durable and timeless enough to be worn not just at the office, but anywhere for years to come. 

These should be wardrobe staples that can be easily taken from your desk to dinner with the quick swap of accessories, a jacket, or blouse. “Keep your work-approved trousers and swap out your top for something a little (or a lot) more fun,” Dickson recommends. “I think if you can make your existing outfit work with slight modifications, that’s always the best."

Style

Trends come and go, but what never goes out of style is a silhouette that you love. “I’m leaning into more relaxed fits and wide leg denim,” says Dickson, echoing a trend we’ve seen on celebrities as of late. No matter if you’re donning jeans or dress pants, you can easily channel these exaggerated styles into your wardrobe in an office-appropriate way.

Spanx’s The Perfect Pant makes the retro wide-leg silhouette perfectly professional, and Express’ Editor Mid-Rise Relaxed Trouser Pants turns your slacks into a modern-day boyfriend pant. And if you lean the other way, there’s straight styles from Banana Republic and even satin trousers — endless options in order to make your office outfits feel like your own.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How do pants sizes work?

    You probably know where you fall on the spectrum of extra small, extra large, etc., but work pants often follow numerical sizes that correlate with your dress size or the measurements of your waist. Many pairs also come in different lengths (called an inseam), and depending on your height, you may have to opt for shorter or longer inseams to best fit your frame. 

    You can measure your waist by wrapping a sewing tape measure around the natural curve of your waist and writing down that number (in inches). Once that’s done, you will place the tape on the inside of your thigh and measure from your groin to your ankle to get your inseam length. With these dimensions in tow, be sure to read the size or fit guide in your favorite pair’s product description online (and reading the reviews never hurt, either).

  • What styles of pants are appropriate for work?

    Nowadays, and especially since the popularization of hybrid work schedules, there aren’t too many rules around what is considered “appropriate” for the workplace. But as a rule of thumb, work pants should usually be free of decorative rips or fraying, not made from sheer materials like chiffon or lace, and a high enough waistband so that your midriff isn’t showing.

  • How can you style work pants for the office?

    For Dickson, shoes are the key. “It’s important to make sure your shoe works with the pant...there is nothing worse than a trouser dragging because the shoe isn’t the right height,” she says. Throwing on a pair of comfortable heels can add that extra height you’re looking for if your hem is just a bit too long. In Dickson’s eye’s the perfect fit is when your hem just skims the floor.

    “I also love a belt as an easy add-on, especially if you’re trying to make a typically more formal pant feel a bit more relaxed,” Dickson adds. However you style your new pair of pants, make sure it’s a way that makes you feel like you’re putting your best foot forward.
