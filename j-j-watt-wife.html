J.J Watt’s Wife: Early Life & Education

Kealia has one elder sister whose name is Megan. Megan is also a soccer player who played for Youth National Team. She was also on the winning team of the 2007 NCAA championship. Megan is the source of inspiration for Kealia.

Education

Kealia Ohai attended Alta High School. She had a great interest in soccer from an early age; thus, she played soccer in school. Besides soccer, Ohai starred on track teams also. She played four years of varsity soccer as a forward in Alta.

Kealia graduated from high school in 2010 by winning various titles. She was 2X Gatorade State Player of the Year, 3X First-Team High School All America, 3X All-Star Selection, 3X 5A soccer MVP, & the 2010 NSCAA National High School Player of the Year.

Her most significant achievement during high school was when she won Parade All-American Honors in 2010.

After graduation from high school, Kealia went to the University of North Carolina Tar Heels. She was a college superstar player. With Tar Heel, Kealia won a national championship (NCCA) title in 2012. She played with North Carolina for three years.

Kealia Ohai: Professional Career

Kealia’s professional journey in soccer started after she was drafted in the 2014 NWSL Draft by Houston Dash as the second overall pick.

In the 2012 NCAA Women’s Soccer Championship, Ohai earned the title of most outstanding offensive player. She was also named All-Atlantic Coast Conference (ACC) and All-Freshman Team (ACC).

She was also on the 2012 FIFA U-20 Women’s World Cup, and the USA team won against Germany. The team USA won by Kealia’s contribution of scoring the game-winning goal.

Similarly, Kealia was also on U.S. Youth National programs where she played with Under-15, Under-17, Under-18, Under-20, and lastly, the Under-23 teams.

Kealia played with Houston Dash from 2014 to 2019, became captain of the team in the 2016 season, and won the NWSL player of the Week.

In January 2020, Kealia was traded to Chicago Red Stars in the 2020 NWSL College Draft.

International Career

Her first international game was with the Senior Unite States Women’s National Soccer Team (USWNT), and the game was against Switzerland.

The match happened on October 23, 2016, in Minneapolis. Kealia showed phenomenal performance by scoring a goal just after 48 seconds of entering the game. Also, she made the history of the fastest goal in USWNT.
